A challenging, top-down arcade racing game where the primary objective is to survive and complete 100 laps. üé± Billiards

This online billiards game is a simulation where your objective is to be the first player to pocket all of your assigned balls (either solids or stripes) and then legally pocket the 8-ball to win. To play, you use your mouse to drag on the table, which both aims your shot and sets the power; the farther you drag, the harder the shot will be. The game assists you by showing a white dashed line for the cue ball's path and a yellow line for the object ball's predicted direction. You release the mouse button to take the shot. The table is open until the first ball is pocketed, which assigns that ball's group (solids or stripes) to the player who made the shot, and your turn continues as long as you legally pocket your own balls.

üí∏ Blackjack

You start with 1,000 chips, and your objective is to reach the goal of 100,000 chips. To play, you begin a round by selecting a bet amount (10, 50, 100, 200, or 500 chips) from the available buttons. The goal of Blackjack is to get a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer without going over. You and the dealer will both be dealt two cards, but one of the dealer's cards will remain hidden. On your turn, you can Hit to take another card or Stand to keep your current hand and end your turn. If your hand's value exceeds 21, you bust and immediately lose your bet. After you stand, the dealer reveals their hidden card and must continue to hit until their hand totals 17 or more. You win if your score is higher than the dealer's or if the dealer busts; a normal win pays 2:1 (doubling your bet), and a "Blackjack" (a 21 on the first two cards) pays 2.5:1. The game ends if you run out of chips or if you successfully reach the 100,000 chip goal.

üí© Shithead Card Game

Welcome to the Shithead card game, where your objective is to be the first player to get rid of all your cards; the last player left with cards is the Shithead. To play, you must place a card of equal or higher value than the top card on the pile, following a card order from 3 (lowest) to Ace (highest). You must play all cards from your hand first, then your face-up cards, and finally your blind face-down cards to win the game.

üí≤ Sokoban Puzzle Game

üíÑ BabeLinux

BabeLinux is an interactive, browser-based terminal simulation that mimics a desktop operating system. Upon loading, the application will prompt you for a username and then run a short boot sequence before presenting you with a command prompt. You can interact with the app by typing commands; a full list is available by typing help. The interface supports a multi-tab environment, allowing you to open new tabs with Alt + T and close them with Alt + X. Key functionalities include nano for a simple text editor, pic for a basic image editor with filters, and play to load and run local audio or video files. The app also includes utilities to change the color theme, view command history, and launch games like snake or tetris.

üß≤ Base64 Generator

Base64 Generator that works for both images and audio files, organized into two separate tabs. To use it, select either the Image or Audio tab and then upload a file by clicking the button or by dragging and dropping it onto the main preview area. If you upload an image, you can use the toolbar controls to apply various adjustments, such as cropping, resizing, changing the JPEG quality, or reducing the number of colors. The app will then generate the Base64 code for the processed image, which you can download as a text file, or you can download the final image as a JPEG. If you upload an audio file, the tool will display its Base64 code, which you can then copy to your clipboard. You also have the options to compress the audio by lowering its sample rate or to play the audio back directly from its Base64 source.

üèúÔ∏è Image Editor

üîí Secret Note

This is a secure, private note-taking application that runs entirely in your web browser. You can type directly on the page and paste both text and images into the editor. When you paste an image, the app converts it into Base64 text-based code, allowing it to be embedded directly within the note's content. To save, click Save Encrypted Note; you will be prompted for a password, which the app uses to strongly encrypt the entire note (including your text and the embedded image code) using AES-GCM before downloading it to your computer as an .encrypted.txt file. To open a saved note, click Open Encrypted Note, select the file, and provide the same password to decrypt and display all your content. All encryption and decryption happen locally on your device, so your password and plain-text data are never sent over the internet.

üé∂ Soundtracker

This application is a Tracker, a type of music sequencer where music is organized on a grid and flows from top to bottom. To create a song, you can either press Play for simple playback or Record to start live recording.
